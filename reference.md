AGODA ASCEND
GenAI Showcase — Conference Proposal
May 2026  |  Confidential Draft

Overview
The Agoda Ascend conference will feature a two-part GenAI showcase designed to leave every attending C-suite executive convinced that AI-powered hospitality is deployable today — and that Agoda is the partner to get them there.
The showcase comprises:
Part 1 — The Showcase Presentation: A presenter-driven HTML slide reel (~25–30 minutes) that narrates a next-generation guest journey from booking to checkout, with live app demos embedded at key moments.
Part 2 — The Sandbox Microsite: A mobile-first web app accessed by QR code that lets attendees experience the AI tools as a guest themselves, at their own pace on their phones.

The Three Pillars
Every element of both deliverables is anchored to three memorable takeaways. These are the three things every attendee should be able to articulate the next morning.
#	Takeaway	Emotional Resonance	Guest Feeling
1	Every guest feels like the only guest	Hyper-personalisation is real and deployable now	I am known
2	The hotel already handled it	Frictionless, proactive service anticipates every problem	I am safe
3	Our people focus on what only humans can do	AI elevates staff to empathy and connection roles	I am cared for

Part 1: The Showcase Presentation
Format
A single HTML interactive slide reel, controlled by the presenter via keyboard or clicker. Each act is a full-screen section with minimal text — visuals and short quotes dominate. Live demo moments are flagged so the presenter knows when to switch to the sandbox app on screen. The reel is offline-capable and does not depend on conference wifi.
Structure
Opening Act — The Tipping Point (Why Now) 
A standalone 2-minute framing section before Elena’s story begins. Three AI capabilities that became real in the last 18 months are named explicitly, each paired with the guest experience it unlocked. This section answers the executive’s unspoken question: “Why is this a 2026 conversation and not a 2028 one?”
1.  Real-time contextual memory across sessions — LLMs can now hold a guest’s full history across properties, stays, and channels without manual data stitching.
2.  Sub-second multi-agent orchestration — Multiple AI agents (booking, housekeeping, maintenance, concierge) coordinate in real time without human dispatch.
3.  Multimodal language understanding at scale — Voice, text, and intent across 23+ languages, interpreted natively without translation loss.
Act I — Discovery & Booking: The Intelligent Negotiation
Pillar: Every guest feels like the only guest
Elena dictates her needs to her personal AI agent from her home office. The hotel’s agentic infrastructure  recognises her high-value status, constructs a custom “Productivity Bundle” via Attribute-Based Booking (quiet high floor, late checkout, park view), and closes the booking without Elena visiting a website or speaking to a human.
Live demo on screen: Attribute-Based Offer Builder — presenter picks a guest persona and the audience watches the AI unbundle room features into a dynamic priced offer in real time.
Act II — Arrival: The Invisible Welcome
Pillar: Every guest feels like the only guest
Elena’s phone is her multipass. She bypasses the front desk entirely. Her room is pre-set to the “Focus Mode” she used at a sister property in Portugal. The smart TV shows her personal itinerary — gym at 6am, dinner based on her organic cuisine preference.
Live demo on screen: Guest Memory & Preference Engine — a mock guest profile card with inferred preferences and transparent AI reasoning behind each one.
Act III — The Stay: Virtual Concierge
Pillar: The hotel already handled it
Elena’s door unlocks and her phone lights up with two proactive nudges the concierge has already prepared: a welcome note flagging that the hotel’s minibar is restocked and the pool closes at 10pm, and a quiet question — “Do you have a local data plan, or would you like me to organise an eSIM for your stay?” She taps one button. Done. Later she asks the virtual concierge to book a sunset kayaking session; it surfaces the hotel’s pre-vetted activity partners with a 10% guest discount already applied. She messages to say she’d like housekeeping at 2pm — the AI checks against the cleaning team’s schedule, confirms the slot fits, and books it. At 2:17pm her phone buzzes: “Your room is freshly made up.”
Live demo on screen: AI Virtual Concierge — presenter opens the chat interface as a guest: triggers the eSIM prompt, books an activity from the partner directory, and schedules housekeeping. The audience watches each action confirm in real time, including the housekeeping completion notification.
Act IV — In Transit: The Journey Continues
Pillar: Our people focus on what only humans can do
The guest is in a taxi to the airport. A notification from the hotel chain lands: “Your airport transfer is confirmed — your driver is 4 minutes away.” A second message follows: “You’re travelling to Singapore tomorrow — have you completed your digital arrival card? We’ve added the link in case it helps.” At Dubai International, with a six-hour layover ahead, the hotel chain’s AI surfaces a targeted offer: a day room at their airport property — shower, lie-down, quiet — for four hours at a discounted rate. No searching, no browsing. The right offer, at the right moment, from a brand the guest already trusts. Every touchpoint after checkout keeps the relationship alive.
Live demo on screen: Post-Checkout Journey Hub — presenter steps through the guest’s transit timeline: transfer confirmation, digital arrival card prompt for the next destination, and the Dubai day-room offer. Each moment shows the AI acting on context the guest never had to provide.
Closing — The Handoff
A QR code fills the screen. “Everything you just watched — you can try it right now, as yourself, on your phone.” The presenter introduces The Grand Neural sandbox and transitions the room from theatre to hands-on exploration.

Part 2: The Sandbox Microsite
Format
A mobile-first Progressive Web App (PWA) accessed via QR code at the conference. No login required beyond a 30-second onboarding. Designed for 3–5 minutes of exploration per app, totalling ~20–30 minutes of engagement for motivated attendees.
The Grand Neural — The Mock Hotel
All sandbox apps share a single fictional property as their context: The Grand Neural. This mock hotel has seeded room inventory, staff, local recommendations, and event spaces. It gives the sandbox coherence — every app the attendee touches feels like it belongs to the same world.
"Be the Guest" Onboarding
On first scan, attendees complete a 30-second onboarding: enter their name, select one travel preference (e.g. “I travel for business”, “I prefer quiet rooms”, “I value local dining”). This seeds a personalised mock guest profile used by every subsequent app — the hotel “knows” them from their first tap.

Success Criteria
The showcase is successful if, at the end of the conference session:
1.  Attendees can articulate all three pillars unprompted.
2.  At least 60% of attendees open the sandbox on their phone during or after the presentation.
3.  The Showcase feels like a story, not a product demo — Elena’s journey is memorable.
4.  No sandbox app requires explanation to use — intuitive within 10 seconds of opening.

Out of Scope (for Launch)
Real hotel PMS/CRS integration (mock data only); real flight API (scripted delay simulation); attendee data persistence beyond the conference session; multilingual sandbox UI (English UI, multilingual content within App 7); 3D event layout rendering in the MICE app (text-based proposal for launch).
